# Strategy

Track long-term fantasy philosophy and current roster plan.

## Team Direction

- Current status: Contender, but not an all-in juggernaut.
- Time horizon: Push for 2026 playoffs while preserving enough 2027+ value to pivot if the team starts slowly.
- Risk tolerance: Moderate. Spend secondary assets and excess depth for starting-lineup upgrades, but avoid moving premium future picks before the season unless the return is a durable top-40 dynasty asset.

## Preferred Roster Build

- Prioritize start-10 weekly points through durable RB/WR/TE starters and high-probability flex depth.
- Consolidate bench/depth pieces into stronger starters when possible.
- Keep enough future liquidity to reassess after Weeks 4-6 before making true all-in moves.

## Player Archetypes I Like

- Young or prime-age players who can improve the starting lineup now and retain dynasty value.
- RBs with immediate workload paths, because usable RB depth matters in this 4-flex lineup.
- WRs with target-earning profiles and first-round draft capital when available at rookie-pick discounts.

## Player Archetypes I Avoid

- Older production-only players when the price requires premium future picks.
- 1QB rookie quarterbacks at a first-round rookie price when my roster already has usable quarterback options.
- Non-TE-premium tight ends at inflated rookie prices unless the value drop is obvious.

## Trade Philosophy

- Do not overpay for replaceable production.
- Prefer deals that improve starting lineup quality or add durable asset value.
- Challenge assumptions from trade calculators.
- Use 2nds, 3rds, bench pieces, and redundant depth to buy points before moving future 1sts.
- Do not move a 2027 1st before the season unless the return is a durable top-40 dynasty asset.
